Should I Drink More Coffee?

This is a valid question that I ask myself every day. While you take your break here are the facts about coffee, caffine, and if you should have more or not.

Coffee contains caffeine, a common and well known stimulant that fights fatigue and increases energy levels. This works for anyone, but is most commonly consumed by students, influencers, full time Moms, and workers who are underpaid.

Caffeine works by blocking one of our bodies sleep-inducing molecules: adenosine. Adenosine slows down our brain signals and is the main reason we get tired throughout the day or before bed. Caffeine disrupts this process, making it so adenosine never tells your body to get tired. Caffeine can also help boost positive feelings. When you get tired by way of adenosine, dopamine has a hard time lifting your mood. While caffeine is blocking adenosine, dopamine can continue telling your body to be happy. These effects have long-term benefits too, reducing the risk of diseases.

The real reason coffee is helpful and students love it so much? It helps you stay awake longer, and you are therefore able to be more productive.

Why does caffeine stop working?

Your brain will start adapting to the caffeine you have been drinking, and create more ways to listen to adenosine. This means that even when you drink caffeine, adenosine will continue to tell your body you need sleep making caffeine less and less effective. Be cautious, caffeine is a drug and withdrawal for caffeine can happen, but only for a few days. You should take a week break from caffeine to restart this process, let your body readjust so you can start drinking caffeine again in lower doses, but feel better and even more awake than before.

I suggest this after finals.

FACT

Research says taking purposeful study breaks (with or without coffee), will help increase your mood, productivity, and ability to focus. Even just five minutes.

How to Take an Effective Break

Set time aside

Study breaks can be missed if you do not actively set time aside for them.

No Social Media

Being on your phone is not an effective break. You need to get your eyes and mind away from a screen and/or small text.

Move your Body

Take a walk around where you are. Taking a shower is also a nice way to feel productive but get your mind away from school.

Get Coffee

Caffeine is a great way to improve your mood and give you extra energy to study a little longer. Coffee is my caffine drink of choice.

Other Ideas

Organize your work space, listen to music, get a snack. If you're with friends, take a break together to talk and laugh. Stretching is also a great thing to do, especially if you have been sitting for hours. Don't be afraid to let your mind just wander, daydreaming is a great way to rest and reset.

Time

It is recommended to take a break from five minutes to one hour. Set a timer for what you feel is appropriate and stick to it. Not only when to take a break, but how long the break is.
